About Me

Hi! I’m Maxwell Miller, but you can call me Max. I have been creating music since 2015. I am originally from Washington State, and grew up in the mountains of Central Washington.






















Why do I write music?

People have many different motivations for doing things.


Semi-Spiritual Experience


Looking back, I think it is more of a necessary thing for me as someone who enjoys music. I have always been fascinated with building and modifying things; learning how they work and go together.


Has been a path which has been impactful for me and I wish to try to give back how I can.













Aesthetics

Well… this is when things get tricky. I am still very much in my youth as a composer, and cannot point to any real niche or style. Instead I would like to share several stories with pieces that demonstrate how those experiences have affected me.
















Story #1: The true and solemn duty of an undergraduate composer.

Things were not going well for me compositionally, and I had a fair amount of friction with my composition instructor.


I had fallen into a trap of doing things which I deemed “correct” and not things that were authentic. I became very concerned about what I “should” be doing and not what I wanted to be doing.


This created a period of frustration for me as I struggled to find my voice, but ended up writing a piece inspired by my struggle.


Writer's Block











Story #2: A study in failure because of course I can do everything all at once.

I had started my masters degree and had been given essentially unlimited creative freedom, backed by very supportive mentors. I got very ambitious with my projects, and began to become infatuated with expanding the scope of my intermedia work.


First time I had received grant funding for “research”.


Despite this the technical demands of performance quickly eclipsed my own capabilities. I learned that there is more to having a piece be successful than having a really good idea. The execution is important.


Flight Manifest











Story #3: Less is more and I’m not happy about it.

I began pulling together my thesis project, and was struck with inspiration (which is certainly not tied to stress in the slightest).










I bought a guitar.










Which is certainly different from the time that I bought all those synthesizers, the pedal steel guitar, the 8 string guitar, the modular synth, the accordion, or the other guitar.










The thesis was titled Existential Crisis (or the Art of Buying a Guitar). I was again going to write something about myself and my experiences, but this time I wasn’t going to go as broad as Flight Manifest.


This was of course a multi-movement, modular, standalone, audio-visual, improvised piece written in two programming languages I had never used before.


Despite these odds stacked against me, I managed to make something more coherent than Flight Manifest, but still struggled with the technical aspects of performance.


Internal Monologue with a Captive Audience


Interestingly enough the middle movement seemed to be the most effective. The piece, titled Antithesis (this is the part where I scream) is actually just the part where I scream. In all of the time I spent building the effects patch for the first and third parts, I had found that it sounded really cool to just scream into a cheap microphone through a bunch of effects.


Antithesis (this is the part where I scream)








Re: Aesthetics

I think it is easiest for me to categorize my work by looking at commonalities across individual works.


Pitch is not super important to me.


I frequently utilize technology, mostly as a means to create a more immersive environment for my audience. I am particularly interested in audio-visual works.


I am interested in music which has some sort of connection to myself or explores vulnerability.


I enjoy being involved in the music I create through improvisation or performance.


All of this is subject to change.
